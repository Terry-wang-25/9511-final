Statutory guarantees and consumer guarantees

Follow the numbered steps in order, then choose whether to submit a claim or contact support.

← Back to faulty goods overview

When to use this path: You bought goods or paid for a service and want to know if the law already promises a minimum standard (quality, match with description, fitness for purpose).

  1. Confirm what you paid for

    Write down the product or service, date, price, and what the ad, label or contract said it would do.

  2. Separate “change of mind” from a fault

    If the item is unsafe, broken, does not work as described or is not fit for what you told the seller you needed, note that as a consumer-law issue—not a store policy preference.

  3. Gather quick proof

    Photos or video of the fault, packaging, model numbers, and any messages where you described your needs to the business.

  4. Contact the seller in one channel

    Email or the official complaints form: state what you bought, what went wrong, what outcome you want (repair, replacement or refund), and a clear deadline for reply.

  5. Keep a dated record

    Save a copy of your message and any reply. You will need this if you escalate or use the checker.
