Access to information

Find out how you can request access to information held by Consumer Rights and the Professional Legal Team in connection with consumer education, complaints triage and referrals.

On this page

Introduction

We aim to be transparent about records that relate to consumer enquiries, workshops, publications and case-tracking workflows. Access may be provided informally (a quick copy of your own submission) or through a formal process where legislation or internal rules apply.

What we may hold

  • contact forms and messages about goods, services, refunds or scams;
  • case tracker updates you or our staff add to a matter;
  • de-identified analytics about which consumer topics are most viewed;
  • training notes or quality checks that reference your enquiry only where necessary and subject to privacy safeguards.

How to make a request

Tips before you apply

Describe the information you want as clearly as you can (for example: “emails I sent via Contact us in March 2026”). Tell us if you need the response in another format. We may ask to verify your identity to protect your account and others’ privacy.

Informal request

For many consumer questions, start with Contact us. We can often return a copy of your own form responses or confirm next steps without a formal application.

Formal access request

Use the formal application form below where you need a comprehensive response or where a statutory or agency-style timeline applies. We will acknowledge receipt and explain any fees, exemptions or redactions—for example, if a document contains another person’s personal information.
