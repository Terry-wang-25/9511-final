Privacy on this site

Consumer Rights is committed to protecting your privacy when you use this website to learn about refunds, warranties, scams, tenancy issues and other consumer topics. This statement explains what information we collect online and how we use it. It should be read together with our full privacy policy (summary below).

What type of information is collected when you visit this site?

When you browse our consumer information pages, our servers may automatically record technical details that help us keep the site secure and improve content. This can include:

  • browser type and version;
  • operating system and device type;
  • referring site or search terms (where available);
  • date, time and approximate duration of your visit;
  • pages and resources you access (for example fact sheets or “Check my rights” steps);
  • anonymised or aggregated network identifiers used for security and performance monitoring.

If you use Contact us, Case tracker or online forms, we collect the personal information you choose to provide (such as name, contact details and a description of your consumer issue) so we can respond or triage your enquiry.

What do we use this information for?

We use visit data for statistical reporting, content planning (for example, which consumer topics need clearer examples) and technical administration. Where possible we work with aggregated or de-identified data and do not attempt to identify individual visitors from server logs alone.

Information you send through forms is used to provide consumer information, referrals or internal training, in line with the consent you give at the time of submission.

What we won’t do with the information

We do not sell your personal information. We do not publish information that could reasonably identify you or your device in open reports. We do not disclose identifying information to third parties except where you have agreed, or where the law requires or permits (for example a serious safety risk or lawful request from a regulator).
