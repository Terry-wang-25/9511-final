Accessibility statement

Consumer Rights is committed to making consumer information accessible to people with disability, older adults, and people with low literacy or limited English. We aim to meet WCAG 2.2 Level AA for new and substantially updated pages.

This site supports:

  • resizeable text and high-contrast navigation patterns inspired by public legal aid sites;
  • keyboard access to primary navigation, forms and overlays where provided;
  • visible focus states on interactive controls;
  • plain-English summaries on complex consumer topics (refunds, warranties, subscriptions) with step-by-step “Check my rights” flows.

We continue to improve captions, heading structure and alternative text on images. If you cannot access part of this site, please tell us using the feedback tile below or Contact us.

Contact the Professional Legal Team

Our team helps with everyday consumer problems—refunds and repairs, misleading prices, subscription traps, unsafe products, scams, energy or telco bills, and rental bond issues.

We can explain your options under Australian consumer law, suggest wording for a complaint to a trader, and point you to the right regulator (for example ACCC, energy ombudsman, or tenancy services) when another agency is better placed to act.

Before you write: gather receipts or bank lines, contract or plan names, dates you were charged, and any emails or texts with the business—this speeds up triage.

Help in other languages

Use the Language menu at the top of every page to change navigation labels where translations are available. Font size controls (A+ / A−) stay next to it so you can read comfortably in any language you choose.

Consumer guides and “Check my rights” steps on this site are written in plain English, with short definitions for words like “warranty”, “chargeback”, and “unfair contract term”. If you need a spoken interpreter, say so in your message on Contact us—we can arrange a return call with a telephone interpreter for longer enquiries where capacity allows.

For quick phrases (for example at a shop counter), you can also open our language overlay from related tools pages to practise switching UI language in the prototype.

Feedback and complaints

Tell us if a page is hard to read, a contrast or focus colour fails for you, a link is broken, or an example (like a refund scenario) should be clearer for people with low literacy or cognitive fatigue.

We log accessibility defects as well as general content corrections. Please include the page title, the device and browser you use, and—if you can—a screenshot or short description of what went wrong.

Complaints about our service (delay, courtesy, or accuracy of information we gave) are handled separately from disputes you have with a trader; use the same Contact us form and choose the closest subject so your message reaches the right team. We aim to acknowledge feedback within 5 business days and to close straightforward fixes within 20 business days where no third party is involved.
